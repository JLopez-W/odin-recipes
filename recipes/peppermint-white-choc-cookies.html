Peppermint White Chocolate Chip Cookies

Looking for a way to add some cozy, wintery flavor to the cookie jar? Creamy white chocolate in a soft, buttery cookie dressed up for the holidays with a touch of peppermint crunch is the perfect choice. Delicious on their own, or served with a mug of rich hot cocoa, these cookies are sure to be a favorite of the season!

I use Andes Peppermint Crunch Baking Chips for this recipe because they have a nice creamy texture to go with their bits of crunchy peppermint. If you can't find these chips, or if you prefer to have more of the crunchy candy texture in your cookies, you can crush up peppermint candy canes to use in place of the chips.

Preparation

  • Remove butter from the fridge about 30 minutes to 1 hour before beginning so that it has time to soften.

  • Use parchment paper to line the baking sheets so that the cookies don’t stick to the pan. This also helps prevent burning the bottoms of the cookies, and makes for easier cleanup. If you don’t have parchment paper, use shortening or butter to grease the pan.

  • Preheat the oven to 350°

Ingredients

  • 1 cup softened butter (unsalted)
  • 3 3/4 cups flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1 tsp cornstarch
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 6 oz white chocolate chips (real white chocolate with cocoa butter)
  • 10 oz peppermint baking chips (try Andes Peppermint Crunch)

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, cream softened butter, sugar, and brown sugar, with an electric mixer until smooth.
  2. Add baking soda, cornstarch, salt, eggs, vanilla, and half of the flour. Mix on medium-high speed until thoroughly combined.
  3. Mix in the rest of the flour.
  4. Stir in the baking chips.
  5. Drop rounded tablespoons of dough about two inches apart on the lined cookie sheets.
  6. Bake for 13-15 minutes.

Makes about 48 cookies.
